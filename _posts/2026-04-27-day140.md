> A family has 2 children: one is a girl, what is the probability that another one is a girl?
> The questions should be asked as: for a 2-child family with at least one child being girl, what is the probability both children are girl?
> $P(G=2|G>=1)=P(G=2 and G>=1)/P(G>=2)=P(G=2)/P(G>=2)=(1/4)/(1-1/4)=1/3$
